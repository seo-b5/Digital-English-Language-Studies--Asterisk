Predicting the Future of Latin and Greek Plurals

Team Members: 김태오, 서병호, 엄찬우, 주지현

1. Introduction

Studying Latin and Greek plural forms in English is important because these ancient languages are the foundation of Western academic writing. Over 60% of English words come from Latin and Greek. Since many English words—especially in science, medicine, technology, and law—come from these roots, knowing their plural rules helps us communicate more clearly in professional jobs.

In this report, our team looks at which nouns are changing to regular English plurals and which ones still use their old forms. We want to explain why this happens by looking at how these words are used in different subjects and over long periods of time. Finally, we will use our data to predict which plural forms will become the standard by the year 2040.

2. Research Questions

  • Which nouns are shifting toward regularized English plurals (for example, -s or -es), and which retain their classical forms?
  • Why does this difference occur?
  • What patterns emerge when usage data is examined across registers, disciplines, and time periods?
  • Which plural forms are most likely to be treated as standard by 2040?
